One of India’s most important wildlife landscapes, famous for one-horned rhinoceroses, elephants, wetlands, and floodplain ecosystems.

State: Assam Focus: Rhino conservation Terrain: Grasslands and wetlands

Madhya Pradesh

Bandhavgarh National Park

A dense forested tiger landscape known for strong big-cat visibility, biodiversity monitoring, and sensitive core-buffer protection planning.

State: Madhya Pradesh Focus: Tiger landscape monitoring Terrain: Forest hills and grassland patches

Uttarakhand

Jim Corbett National Park

One of India's oldest national parks, known for tiger habitat, riverine belts, sal forest, and high-value patrol and eco-monitoring operations.

State: Uttarakhand Focus: Tiger and habitat patrol Terrain: River valleys and forest

Sundarbans National Park

A globally significant mangrove ecosystem where estuarine waterways, islands, and tiger habitat demand careful monitoring and rapid response.

State: West Bengal Focus: Mangrove ecosystem protection Terrain: Delta creeks and islands
